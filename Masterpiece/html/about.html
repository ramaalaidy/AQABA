Location & Geography

Located in the southernmost part of Jordan, Aqaba is bordered by Saudi Arabia, Palestine, and Egypt.

It serves as a key transit point for tourists heading to Wadi Rum and Petra.

History & Culture

Aqaba is an ancient city with a history spanning over 5,000 years, once a major Silk Road hub.

It boasts archaeological sites such as Aqaba Castle, the Islamic ruins of Ayla, and the Aqaba Museum.

How to Get to Aqaba?

By Air: King Hussein International Airport.

By Car: About a 4-hour drive from Amman via the desert road.

By Boat: Accessible via cruises from Egypt.
